A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015 was an Indianacrospace scientist who serverd as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born annd raised in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and arospace engineering. He spent the next four decade as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Orgnisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Orgnisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development effforts. [1] He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. [2][3][4] He is also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhar-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by india in 1974.
